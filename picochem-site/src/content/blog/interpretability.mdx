## Methodology

A short summary of the interpretability methods I'll use: linear probes,
attention-pattern inspection, and ablation studies. Each is paired with a
faithfulness check so we don't over-read the results.

## Linear probing

Train a linear classifier on each layer's hidden states for a handful of
chemically meaningful targets: ring count, aromaticity, presence of a
stereocenter, halogen identity.

## Faithfulness

A linear probe says a feature is *decodable*, not that the model *uses*
it. To check faithfulness, ablate the probe direction at inference and see
whether downstream behavior actually changes.<Footnote id="1">See e.g. Elazar et al., "Amnesic Probing" (2021) — projecting out the probe direction is a much stronger test than the probe accuracy alone.</Footnote>

## Failure taxonomy

Where the model breaks, qualitatively:
- Heavily fused polycyclics (locant disagreement)
- Long aliphatic chains beyond training length
- Rare halogen substitution patterns
